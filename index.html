Our Story

A World Away,
Right Here in Obs.

This Ethiopian restaurant serves traditional cuisine in a quirky, object-filled house atmosphere. The small establishment specialises in vegetable dishes — or rich combinations of vegetables and meat including lamb and chicken — all served on injera, the soft spongy Ethiopian bread that functions as both plate and utensil.

Relocated from Long Street to its current Observatory home — a building that once housed a pub — the interior is stuffed with vintage African masks, sculptures, and curios, many available for purchase. Classic jazz and blues from Big Mama Thornton and Louis Armstrong fills the air during meals.

The café holds only a few tables, each with a distinct character, making for an intimate dining experience. Outside, cozy chairs on a terrace invite you to linger longer. A traditional Ethiopian coffee ceremony is always available.
